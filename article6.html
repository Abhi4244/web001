Time travel by train

by Deb palmernov 1st, 2023


For some of you, riding on a train might be just a means to get from point A to point B. For me, trains are much more.My first experience on a train was in the early seventies. For $10, (round trip, if I remember 50 years ago correctly), I could travel home from Yakima to Auburn, Washington.In those days, we dressed for travel, expecting to be treated as a welcomed guest, and train treks were simply luxurious. In the dining car, Porters dressed in starched, white linen, referred to me as ma’am, while serving tea from a silver teapot.

Upon taking the last bite of a delightful meal, the Porter would appear with a pressed linen towel draped over one arm to swiftly remove plates and brush crumbs off the tablecloth with a silver brush and tray.After breakfast, I’d retire to the Club Car. It didn’t take long to figure out they had no problem serving me cocktails, underage or not. No one ever asked for my ID which tickled my mischievous side as normally people thought I was younger than my 18 years. So, because I could, I ordered a Screwdriver (vodka and OJ) with an umbrella, please, and the Porter smiled and said, “Yes, ma’am, one Screwdriver with an umbrella coming up.”

The trip was only about 3 hours long, but I, being such a romantic child, grazed a lifetime of dreams with every minute. I’d pull out my yellow legal pad, place it next to my illegal cocktail, and let my ballpoint pen take me on grand adventures. The wildest stretch of imagination was the pretense that I was a real writer. In my mind, those passing the young woman with the umbrella-graced cocktail mistook her for a famous author they couldn’t quite place.
